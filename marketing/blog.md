# Your computer, but actually global

Most of the web is built for tabs. Open a thing, do a thing, close the tab, forget it ever happened.

**GlobalOS** is built for something older and more human: a **desk**. A place you sit down at, leave things open, come back tomorrow, and pick up where you left off. Except this desk isn’t tied to one laptop, one browser profile, or one afternoon. It lives on your **Global PC**.

---

## One machine. Many desks.

When you sign in to GlobalOS, you’re not landing in yet another dashboard. You’re landing on **your Global PC** — a persistent environment that’s yours across sessions, devices, and time.

On that machine you can keep **multiple workspaces**: separate desks for separate modes of work. A “main” desk with your everyday tools. An experiment desk you don’t want cluttering the rest. A scratch desk for a side project. Each workspace remembers **which apps were open and where you put them**. Close the browser tab. Log in again next week. Your layout is still there.

That’s the first promise: **continuity**. The web usually treats every visit like a first visit. GlobalOS treats every visit like coming back to *your* room.

---

## Real apps, not bookmarks

The icons on your desktop aren’t shortcuts to websites. They’re **`.gapp` applications** — self-contained programs that run inside your Global PC, with windows, state, and behavior.

Open a text editor. It restores what you were working on.  
Open a file browser. It’s a real tool wired into *your* files, not a demo pane.  
Launch something new. It opens in a window you can move, resize, and stack — because you’re not “using a site,” you’re **using a computer**.

You get the familiarity of a desktop without the ritual of installs, updates, or “which machine was that file on?”

---

## The desktop metaphor, without the baggage

GlobalOS looks like a desktop on purpose. Humans already know how to work that way: icons, windows, focus, clutter, flow.

But underneath, it’s built for how people actually live now:

- **Works in the browser** — no download, no IT ticket, no “supported platforms” list
- **Your files and apps stay with your account** — not scattered across a dozen SaaS tabs
- **Each app runs in its own isolated environment** — so one program can’t casually read another’s cookies or impersonate the shell

You get the *feeling* of a personal computer with the *reach* of the web.

---

## A shell that works for you, not against you

Ever notice how most web apps fight the browser? They each invent their own save button, their own file picker, their own idea of “open.”

GlobalOS flips that. The platform provides a consistent layer — open, save, browse files, launch programs — so apps can focus on what makes them interesting. You’re not learning a new UX language every time you click an icon.

For you, that means less “where does this app keep my stuff?” and more “I know where I am: **my Global PC, this workspace, these windows.**”

---

## Who is this for?

**GlobalOS is for people who live in the browser but miss having a place.**

- Creators who want a stable desk for tools and works-in-progress
- Tinkerers who want to run little apps without provisioning a whole dev environment
- Anyone tired of reopening the same seventeen tabs after every restart

It’s not trying to replace your operating system tomorrow. It’s offering something the web has been bad at for decades: **a home**.
